## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Computer Numerically Controlled (CNC) Tool Operator
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 51-9161.00 — Computer Numerically Controlled Tool Operators
- **Related occupation(s):** 51-4031.00 Cutting, Punching & Press Machine Setters/Operators (Metal & Plastic)
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=51-9161.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice CNC/Machine Operator** — supervised setup & operation; safety, LOTO, blueprint basics
2. **Journeyworker (Journeyman) Operator** — independent setup, program editing, first-article verification
3. **Lead / Master Operator** — process ownership, fixturing/nesting optimization, OJT mentor

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~2,000–4,000 OJL hours (1–2 years, time-based)
- On-the-job learning + ~144 hrs/yr Related Technical Instruction (RTI)
- Completion credential: journey-level CNC Tool Operator

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Strong candidate for an in-house Registered Apprenticeship; RTI can be delivered with a local technical college or NIMS coursework.
- Tie journey-level sign-off to the certs in `CERTIFICATIONS.md` (NIMS, OEM waterjet operator).
