# Mechanical & Electro-Mechanical Assembly — Certifications & Qualifications

**Subsidiary:** Tyonek Manufacturing Group, Inc.
**Work Process:** Mechanical & Electro-Mechanical Assembly
**Last updated:**

> **Legend** — This is a U.S. Government contract environment. All regulatory, statutory, and contractually-flowed requirements are listed as **Required**.
> - **Required** — mandated by law, regulation, contract, or needed to legally/safely perform the work.
> - **Recommended** — industry standard; strengthens quality, compliance, and competitiveness.
> - **Nice-to-have** — broadens capability or supports career development.

## Required
- **ANSI/ESD S20.20** ESD control handling (electro-mechanical)
- Calibrated torque tool qualification / torque control training
- Internal assembly operator qualification & work-instruction sign-off (AS9100)
- Hazard Communication (adhesives, sealants, potting) — OSHA 1910.1200
- PPE qualification

## Recommended
- IPC-A-610 (acceptability, where soldered interconnects present)
- NASA-STD-8739 workmanship series — *Required* if the contract is NASA hardware
- GD&T / blueprint reading
- Crimp & connector qualification (M22520)

## Nice-to-have
- Six Sigma Green Belt / lean manufacturing
- IPC/WHMA-A-620 (cross-training with harness)
- Foreign Object Debris/Damage (FOD) prevention program lead
