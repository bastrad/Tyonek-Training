# Coatings, Paint & Markings — Certifications & Qualifications

**Subsidiary:** Tyonek Manufacturing Group, Inc.
**Work Process:** Coatings, Paint & Markings
**Last updated:**

> **Legend** — This is a U.S. Government contract environment. All regulatory, statutory, and contractually-flowed requirements are listed as **Required**.
> - **Required** — mandated by law, regulation, contract, or needed to legally/safely perform the work.
> - **Recommended** — industry standard; strengthens quality, compliance, and competitiveness.
> - **Nice-to-have** — broadens capability or supports career development.

## Required
- Respiratory Protection program & fit test — OSHA 1910.134
- Isocyanate (polyurethane/CARC) exposure training — OSHA
- Hazard Communication (paints, solvents, primers) — OSHA 1910.1200
- RCRA hazardous waste handling (spent solvents, paint waste) — EPA 40 CFR 260–279
- EPA VOC / National Emission Standards (NESHAP 6H) compliance for coatings
- Chemical Agent Resistant Coating (**CARC**) application qualification — *Required* for military ground-system finishes
- **MIL-STD-130** item marking & identification (UID) compliance
- NADCAP chemical processing / coatings compliance (facility accreditation)

## Recommended
- AMPP (formerly NACE/SSPC) Coating Inspector Level 1
- Certified aerospace/industrial spray painter qualification
- Powder-coat / e-coat application certification (if used)

## Nice-to-have
- AMPP Coating Inspector Level 2
- Laser marking system operator certification
- Confined space entry (tanks/booths) — OSHA 1910.146
