## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Wire & Cable Harness Assembler
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 51-2028.00 — Electrical, Electronic & Electromechanical Assemblers, Except Coil Winders
- **Related occupation(s):** 49-2022.00 Telecommunications Equipment Installers & Repairers (fiber optic pathway); 17-3023.00 Electronic Eng. Technicians
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Assembler; Fiber Optic Technician emerging)
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=51-2028.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice Harness Assembler** — wire prep/marking, crimp basics, ESD, IPC/WHMA-A-620 intro
2. **Journeyman Assembler (A-620 certified)** — copper harness build & test; fiber termination/inspection
3. **Master / CIT / Fiber Specialist (FOA CFOS) / Lead** — complex harnesses, ARINC fiber, trains operators

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~2,000–4,000 OJL hours (1–2 years, time-based)
- On-the-job learning + ~144 hrs/yr Related Technical Instruction (RTI)
- Completion credential: journey-level Harness Assembler + IPC/WHMA-A-620 and fiber certs

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Two credential tracks converge here (A-620 for copper, FOA for fiber) — see `CERTIFICATIONS.md`.
- Fiber optic technician is a strong emerging DOL apprenticeship worth registering separately if volume warrants.
