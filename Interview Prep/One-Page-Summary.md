## The opportunity
Tyonek's subsidiaries span **aerospace manufacturing, aviation MRO, cyber/mission support, and
construction** — each a heavily regulated, government-contract environment where **workforce
qualification directly drives compliance, audit outcomes, retention, and contract performance.**
The Training Manager owns that across every subsidiary and work process.

## My approach — a working framework, not a theory
Ahead of today, I built a prototype training-program architecture covering **all four
subsidiaries and their 46 work processes.** Built from public capability statements and public
standards, designed to be validated against your actual contracts and current practice. It:

- **Organizes training by lean value streams** — focus effort on what delivers customer value,
  with quality built in as a station in every business.
- **Maps every requirement to the rule that drives it** — OSHA, AS9100/AS9110, FAA Part 145,
  DCMA 8210.1C, DoD 8570/8140, NISPOM, EM 385-1-1 — so we stay **audit-ready by design.**
- **Builds career pathways** — each role mapped to its DOL/O*NET trade with an
  **Apprentice → Journeyman → Master** progression, including where we could register formal
  DOL apprenticeships (a retention and workforce-pipeline win).
- **Runs as a continuous-improvement loop** — **Verify → Develop → Train → Assess → Document →
  Audit** (PDCA), so the program stays current as contracts and standards change.

## First 90 days
- **Days 0–30 — Learn the real state.** Pull contract/SOW training flow-downs, review current
  records, and meet the process owners. *(Requirements verification.)*
- **Days 31–60 — Find the gaps.** Run a gap analysis per process; prioritize the highest
  compliance-risk gaps; identify which training material must be developed.
- **Days 61–90 — Operationalize.** Stand up the improvement loop and a metrics dashboard;
  scope the first one or two DOL apprenticeship programs.

## How we'll measure success
Certification currency %, training completion %, time-to-qualify, audit-finding closure time,
apprentices advancing tiers, and retention / fill rates.

Initiative, systems thinking, government-contract compliance fluency, lean / continuous-
improvement discipline, and modern operational tooling (version-controlled training governance).
