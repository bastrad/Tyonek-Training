# 14 CFR § 145.163 — Training Requirements (FAA Repair Station)

**Status:** Public domain (U.S. federal regulation) · **Applies to:** Tyonek Services Group (MRO)

## Requirement (summary of paragraphs)
- **(a)** A certificated repair station must have and use an **employee training program
  approved by the FAA** consisting of **initial and recurrent training**.
- **(b)** The training program must ensure each employee assigned to perform maintenance,
  preventive maintenance, alterations, and inspection functions is **capable of performing the
  assigned task**. *(Note: "capability" here is treated as synonymous with "competency.")*
- **(c)** The repair station must **document employee training** in a format acceptable to the
  FAA, and **retain training records for a minimum of 2 years**.
- **(d)** Any **revision** to the training program must be submitted to the responsible Flight
  Standards office per established procedures.

## Training-management implications for Tyonek
- The MRO must maintain an **FAA-approved** training program (not just internal training).
- Both **initial and recurrent** training are mandatory; build recurrence into the loop cadence.
- **Records retention ≥ 2 years** — set this in the records-retention standard work.
- Portions may be met via trade/technical seminars and **on-the-job training** (per AC 145-10).
