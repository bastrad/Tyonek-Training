# Engineering & Design — Trade Classification & Progression

**Subsidiary:** Tyonek Manufacturing Group, Inc.
**Work Process:** Engineering & Design (CATIA, SolidWorks, AutoCAD, Adobe 3D)
**Last updated:**

> company can align to or sponsor a Registered Apprenticeship where one applies. Confirm details
> at apprenticeship.gov and with the state/DOL apprenticeship agency.

## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Mechanical Drafter / Design Technician (trade); Mechanical Engineer (professional)
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 17-3013.00 — Mechanical Drafters
- **Related occupation(s):** 17-3027.00 Mechanical Eng. Technologists & Technicians; 17-2141.00 Mechanical Engineers (licensure)
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable as **Drafter / Design Drafter / Engineering Technician**
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=17-3013.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** Moderate (drafter/technician = apprenticeable; engineer = degree + PE licensure)

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice / Junior Drafter** — CAD fundamentals, GD&T, drawing standards, model management
2. **Journeyman Designer / Design Drafter** — independent build-to-print design, TDP support, checking
3. **Lead Designer / Engineer (PE) / Master** — design authority, engineering analysis, mentors drafters

## Typical Structure
- Drafter apprenticeship term: ~4,000–8,000 OJL hours + ~144 hrs/yr RTI
- Engineer track: ABET degree → EIT/FE → Professional Engineer (PE) licensure (not apprenticeship)
- Completion credential: journey-level Drafter/Designer; or PE for the engineering track

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Register a **Drafter/Design Technician** apprenticeship for the technician tier; keep the PE path as a separate professional ladder.
- Software certs (CATIA/SolidWorks) in `CERTIFICATIONS.md` make good RTI milestones.
