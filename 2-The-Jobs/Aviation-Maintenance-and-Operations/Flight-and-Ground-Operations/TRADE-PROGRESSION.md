# Flight & Ground Operations — Trade Classification & Progression

**Subsidiary:** Tyonek Services Group, Inc.
**Work Process:** Flight & Ground Operations (DCMA-Approved Procedures)
**Last updated:**

> Maps this job scope to recognized classifications. This scope blends a skilled trade
> (ground operators = aircraft mechanics) with a licensure profession (pilots), governed
> operationally by DCMA 8210.1C rather than by a single apprenticeship.

## Trade Classification
- **Ground operations — trade:** Aircraft Mechanic (engine run/taxi qualified)
  - **O*NET-SOC:** 49-3011.00 — Aircraft Mechanics and Service Technicians
  - **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (see Maintenance folders)
- **Flight operations — profession:** Commercial/Test Pilot
  - **O*NET-SOC:** 53-2012.00 — Commercial Pilots
  - **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Emerging pilot apprenticeships; primarily FAA-certificate-based
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=49-3011.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** Moderate (ground = trade ladder; flight = licensure ladder)

## Progression Path
- **Ground operators:** Apprentice Mechanic → Journeyman (engine run/taxi qualified) → Ground Ops Lead
- **Pilots:** Commercial → ATP / Functional-Check-Flight → Test Pilot (per DCMA 8210.1C)

## Typical Structure
- Qualification-based under **DCMA 8210.1C**; no single registered apprenticeship covers the whole scope
- Related instruction: DCMA 8210.1C, movement-area driving, CRM/human factors

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Register the ground-operator qualifications inside the Aircraft-Mechanic apprenticeship; track the pilot path as a professional/licensure ladder.
