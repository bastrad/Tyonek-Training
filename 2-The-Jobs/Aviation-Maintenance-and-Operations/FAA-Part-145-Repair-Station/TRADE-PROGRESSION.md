# FAA Part 145 Repair Station Operations — Trade Classification & Progression

**Subsidiary:** Tyonek Services Group, Inc.
**Work Process:** FAA Part 145 Certified Repair Station Operations
**Last updated:**

> Maps this job scope to the recognized U.S. DOL skilled-trade classification and its
> Apprentice → Journeyworker (Journeyman) → Master/Advanced progression. Note: Part 145 is a
> facility certification; the people working under it hold the aircraft-mechanic trade.

## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Aircraft Mechanic (Repair Station)
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 49-3011.00 — Aircraft Mechanics and Service Technicians
- **Related occupation(s):** 49-2091.00 Avionics Technicians; management/accountable-manager roles
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Aircraft Mechanic) operating under the Part 145 certificate
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=49-3011.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice Mechanic** — works under supervision within Repair Station procedures (RSQM)
2. **Journeyman A&P Mechanic** — return-to-service authority within Op-Specs
3. **Master / Inspector (IA) / Accountable Manager** — quality/regulatory oversight, mentors apprentices

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~8,000 OJL hours (A&P base) delivered within the Part 145 quality system
- Related instruction: RSQM, Op-Specs, 14 CFR Part 120 drug/alcohol, AS9110
- Completion credential: FAA A&P = journey-level

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- The Repair Station itself is an ideal registered-apprenticeship host — the RSQM already documents training/qualification control.
