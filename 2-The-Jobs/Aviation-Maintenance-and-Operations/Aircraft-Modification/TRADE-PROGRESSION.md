## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Aircraft Mechanic / Aircraft Structures Mechanic
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 49-3011.00 — Aircraft Mechanics and Service Technicians
- **Related occupation(s):** 51-2011.00 Aircraft Structure, Surfaces, Rigging & Systems Assemblers; 47-2211.00 Sheet Metal Workers
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Aircraft Mechanic; Aircraft Structures)
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=51-2011.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice Structures/Mod Mechanic** — sheet-metal basics, fastening, wiring, working to approved data
2. **Journeyman Mechanic** — installs modifications per STC/EO/TWO, integrates systems, documents as-modified
3. **Master / Lead / Inspector** — mod planning, DER-data coordination, sign-off, mentors apprentices

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~6,000–8,000 OJL hours (3–4 years) — aligns with A&P and structures experience
- On-the-job learning + ~144 hrs/yr Related Technical Instruction (RTI)
- Completion credential: FAA A&P + structures qualification = journey-level

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Can register as Aircraft Mechanic or as Aircraft Structures Assembler depending on the work mix.
- Sheet-metal (47-2211.00) is a fully apprenticeable sub-trade if fabrication volume warrants a dedicated program.
