## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Electronics Assembler
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 51-2028.00 — Electrical, Electronic & Electromechanical Assemblers, Except Coil Winders
- **Related occupation(s):** 17-3023.00 Electrical & Electronic Engineering Technicians (advancement)
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Assembler, Electronics / Electronics Technician)
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=51-2028.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice Electronics Assembler** — ESD, hand soldering, component ID, workmanship basics
2. **Journeyman Assembler (IPC-certified)** — J-STD-001 / IPC-A-610 production soldering, SMT & through-hole
3. **Master / Certified IPC Trainer (CIT) / Lead** — rework/repair (7711/7721), trains & certifies operators

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~2,000–4,000 OJL hours (1–2 years, time-based)
- On-the-job learning + ~144 hrs/yr Related Technical Instruction (RTI)
- Completion credential: journey-level Electronics Assembler + IPC certifications

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Journey levels map cleanly to the IPC ladder (CIS → CIT) in `CERTIFICATIONS.md`.
- In-house IPC Certified Trainer can deliver the RTI portion.
