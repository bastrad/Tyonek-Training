## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Electromechanical Assembler
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 51-2028.00 — Electrical, Electronic & Electromechanical Assemblers, Except Coil Winders
- **Related occupation(s):** 51-2092.00 Team Assemblers; 51-2031.00 Engine & Other Machine Assemblers
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Assembler; Electromechanical Technician)
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=51-2028.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice Assembler** — torque control, ESD, fastening, work-instruction discipline, FOD control
2. **Journeyman Assembler** — complex electro-mechanical builds, integration, in-process verification
3. **Master / Lead Assembler** — build planning, tooling/fixturing, trains & signs off apprentices

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~2,000–4,000 OJL hours (1–2 years, time-based)
- On-the-job learning + ~144 hrs/yr Related Technical Instruction (RTI)
- Completion credential: journey-level Assembler

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Good "gateway" trade for new hires; can ladder into CCA or harness apprenticeships.
- Journey sign-off aligns with torque, ESD, and IPC-A-610 items in `CERTIFICATIONS.md`.
