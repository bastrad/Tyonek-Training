## Trade Classification
- **Primary occupation:** Occupational Health & Safety Specialist (Construction)
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 19-5011.00 — Occupational Health and Safety Specialists
- **Related occupation(s):** 19-5012.00 Occupational Health & Safety Technicians
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Emerging (Safety Technician apprenticeships exist)
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=19-5011.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** Moderate (technician tier apprenticeable; specialist tier credential-based)

## Progression Path
1. **Safety Technician (OSHA 30)** — site inspections, JHAs, toolbox talks, competent-person tasks
2. **Site Safety & Health Officer / SSHO (CHST)** — runs the EM 385 safety program, 40-hr SSHO qualified
3. **Safety Manager (CSP/CIH)** — corporate safety leadership, mentors technicians

## Typical Structure
- Safety Technician apprenticeship (~2,000–4,000 OJL hrs + RTI) for the technician tier
- Credential ladder: OSHA 30 → BCSP CHST/STSC → BCSP ASP → CSP

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- EM 385-1-1 40-hr SSHO (+ 8-hr refresher) is required for federal-project safety officers — a core RTI/credential gate in `CERTIFICATIONS.md`.
