## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Painter / Coater, Transportation & Manufacturing
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 51-9124.00 — Coating, Painting & Spraying Machine Setters, Operators & Tenders
- **Related occupation(s):** 51-9122.00 Painters, Transportation Equipment
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Painter; Painter, Transportation Equipment)
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=51-9124.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice Painter/Coater** — surface prep, masking, respirator/PPE, HazCom, booth operation
2. **Journeyman Painter/Coater** — spec-compliant coatings (incl. CARC), film-build control, MIL-STD-130 marking
3. **Master / Coating Inspector (AMPP) / Lead** — application engineering, inspection, mentors apprentices

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~2,000–6,000 OJL hours (2–3 years, time-based)
- On-the-job learning + ~144 hrs/yr Related Technical Instruction (RTI)
- Completion credential: journey-level Painter/Coater

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Journey level aligns with CARC qualification and AMPP coating-inspector track in `CERTIFICATIONS.md`.
- Heavy regulatory content (respiratory, isocyanate, RCRA) makes RTI a strong compliance vehicle.
