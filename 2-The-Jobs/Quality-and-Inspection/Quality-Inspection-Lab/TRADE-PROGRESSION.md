## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Quality Control Inspector
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 51-9061.00 — Inspectors, Testers, Sorters, Samplers, and Weighers
- **Related occupation(s):** 17-3028.00 Calibration Technologists & Technicians; 17-3027.00 Mechanical Eng. Technicians
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Quality Control Inspector; Inspector)
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=51-9061.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice Inspector** — measurement tools, GD&T reading, inspection records, stamp discipline
2. **Journeyman QC Inspector** — CMM/FARO programming & operation, First Article (AS9102), MRB support
3. **Master / Quality Engineer / Lead Auditor** — metrology oversight, RCCA, AS9100 auditing, mentors inspectors

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~4,000 OJL hours (2 years, time-based)
- On-the-job learning + ~144 hrs/yr Related Technical Instruction (RTI)
- Completion credential: journey-level QC Inspector + ASQ certifications

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Journey levels ladder onto ASQ CQI → CQT → CQE in `CERTIFICATIONS.md`.
- Metrology/calibration content (ISO 17025) is a strong RTI module.
