# 49 CFR § 172.704 — Training Requirements (DOT Hazmat)

**Status:** Public domain (U.S. federal regulation) · **Applies to:** any subsidiary that ships/handles dangerous goods
**Authoritative / verbatim source:** https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-49/subtitle-B/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-172/subpart-H/section-172.704
**Also:** https://www.law.cornell.edu/cfr/text/49/172.704 · Guidance: https://www.phmsa.dot.gov/training/hazmat/training-requirements-industry

## Required training components (summary)
- **General awareness / familiarization** — familiarity with the subchapter; ability to
  recognize and identify hazardous materials.
- **Function-specific** — the subchapter requirements applicable to the functions the employee
  actually performs.
- **Safety training** — emergency response info, workplace protection measures, and methods/
  procedures for avoiding accidents.
- **Security awareness** — recognizing security risks and how to enhance security; new hazmat
  employees must receive it **within 90 days** of employment.
- **In-depth security training** — required where a security plan applies (company security
  objectives, structure, and specific procedures).

## Frequency
- **Recurrent training at least once every 3 years.**
- A **new employee (or one changing job functions)** may perform functions **under direct
  supervision** of a trained employee provided training is completed **within 90 days**.
- In-depth security training re-occurs every 3 years, or within 90 days of a revised security plan.

## Training-management implications for Tyonek
- Anyone who **prepares, offers, or transports** hazmat is a "hazmat employee" and must be trained.
- Build the **3-year recurrence** into the cadence; track the 90-day new-hire clock.
- Note: where **RCRA** (40 CFR 262.17) also applies (Large Quantity Generators), an **annual**
  refresher may drive a tighter schedule than DOT's 3 years — the stricter rule governs.
