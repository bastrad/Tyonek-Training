# The Companies

Tyonek's four subsidiaries are the scope of the training program. The jobs in
[Phase 2](../2-The-Jobs/) are grouped by trade/discipline rather than by company, but each job
notes its company. Here's who they are.

## Tyonek Manufacturing Group, Inc. — Manufacturing
Builds OEM-quality mechanical and electrical assemblies, components, and kits for aviation,
missile, and ground systems. Vertically integrated with special processes certified to
**ISO 9001:2015, AS9100, and OEM** standards. Capabilities: 3/4/5-axis CNC machining, waterjet,
welding, coatings/paint/markings, circuit card assembly (CCA/SMT), copper & fiber cable harness,
and a quality lab (CMM, FARO, laser scan). Facilities in Madison & Huntsville, AL and
Warner Robins, GA.

## Tyonek Services Group, Inc. — Services (MRO)
Aircraft engineering, modification, maintenance, and testing at MRO facilities (245,000 sq ft)
at Huntsville International (HSV) and Stennis International (HSA), able to induct aircraft as
large as the C-130. Certified to **ISO 9001:2015, AS9100, AS9110, and DCMA 8210.1C**; operates
**FAA Part 145** repair stations. Supports fixed- and rotary-wing platforms for all four
military services, USCG, CBP, and USDA.

## Tyonek Mission Support — Mission Support
*(Legal entity: Tyonek Services Group, Inc.)* Tailored mission support for U.S. Government cyber
operations, special operations, training & exercise support, network security, IT/software,
strategic planning, and engineering. Deep expertise with **U.S. Air Force and USSOCOM** programs;
strong fill/retention rates; ISD/ADDIE courseware and cyber-range engineering capabilities.

## Tyonek Contractor Services, LLC — Construction
Anchorage, Alaska general contractor established 2017; **SBA 8(a)** participant since 2019;
100% minority-owned small business. Performs negotiated/design-build and federal, state, local,
and private construction projects across Alaska and beyond.
