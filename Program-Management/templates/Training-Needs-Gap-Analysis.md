> ## Part 1 — Read this first (plain-language primer)
>
> **What is this document?** It is a two-sided comparison. On one side is **what training the
> law and our customers require** for this job. On the other side is **what the company is
> actually doing today**. The difference between the two is a **"gap."** This document finds
> those gaps so we can fix them.
>
> **Two simple definitions:**
> - **Training need** = a piece of training that a rule says people in this job *must* have.
> - **Gap** = a training need that is *not yet being met* (missing, outdated, or unverified).
>
> **Why is the "current-state" side blank right now?** We are building this **from scratch**.
> The *required* side comes from a known list (Part 2), but we do **not yet have the data on
> what the company is currently doing**. That data must be collected from the actual work site,
> training records, and managers (see Part 3). Until then, the gap columns stay **"PENDING."**
> This is normal — you cannot measure a gap until you know both sides.
>
> **How to use this document (3 steps):**
> 1. **Build the required list.** From the sources in Part 2, list every training this job must have.
> 2. **Collect current-state data.** Use the Part 3 checklist to gather what the company does now.
> 3. **Fill the worksheet (Part 4) and find the gaps.** Compare required vs. current, mark each
>    row, and route every gap to an action (train, develop new material, or fix records).
>
> **Mini-glossary (plain terms):**
> | Term | Plain meaning |
> |---|---|
> | **Required** | The law or the contract says you *must* have it. Not optional. |
> | **Recommended** | Strongly advised; improves quality/compliance but not legally forced. |
> | **Nice-to-have** | Adds skill or growth; not needed to do the job safely/legally. |
> | **Initial training** | The first-time training before someone does the task. |
> | **Recurrent / refresher** | Repeat training on a schedule (e.g., every year) to stay current. |
> | **Competence** | Proof a person can actually *do* the task correctly — not just attend a class. |
> | **OJT** | On-the-Job Training — learning by doing the real work under supervision. |
> | **SME** | Subject-Matter Expert — the experienced person who knows the job. |

## Part 2 — Build the Required List (where it comes from)

You don't invent the required list — you pull it from two places:

1. **The governing-standards catalog** →
   [`References/Governing-Standards-Reference.md`](../../References/Governing-Standards-Reference.md).
   This is the single, plain-language catalog of every rule that mandates training (OSHA,
   EPA/DOT, ISO 9001 / AS9100 / AS9110, FAA Part 145, IPC/AWS, NISPOM / DoD 8570, EM 385-1-1,
   and more). Select the rows whose **"Applies to"** tag matches this subsidiary.
2. **This job's own certification list** → the `CERTIFICATIONS.md` file in this work
   process's folder (the job-specific certs and qualifications).

Together, those two give you the complete **required** side. List each item as a row in the
Part 4 worksheet.

> Not every rule applies to every job — a welder and a cyber analyst have different rules. Use
> the **Applies to** column in the catalog to pick only what's relevant.

## Part 3 — Current-State Data to Collect (do this before the gap analysis)

The gap columns stay **PENDING** until this data is gathered. Collect it from the work site
(Gemba), the training records, and the process owner/SME.

**Checklist — for this work process, obtain:**
- ▢ List of employees performing this work (and their roles)
- ▢ Existing training records / certificates on file (and their dates/expirations)
- ▢ Any existing training materials, lesson plans, or work instructions in use
- ▢ Who currently delivers the training (and whether they are qualified to)
- ▢ How competency is currently checked (test? demonstration? nothing?)
- ▢ Which certifications staff currently hold vs. the required list from Part 2
- ▢ Records-retention method (where records live, how long kept)
- ▢ Any recent audit findings or customer/contract requirements affecting this process
