# Safety (OSHA / EM 385) — Certifications & Qualifications

**Subsidiary:** Tyonek Contractor Services, LLC
**Work Process:** Safety (OSHA / EM 385)
**Last updated:**

> **Legend** — This is a U.S. Government contract environment. All regulatory, statutory, and contractually-flowed requirements are listed as **Required**.
> - **Required** — mandated by law, regulation, contract, or needed to legally/safely perform the work.
> - **Recommended** — industry standard; strengthens quality, compliance, and competitiveness.
> - **Nice-to-have** — broadens capability or supports career development.

## Required
- **OSHA 30-Hour Construction** — 29 CFR 1926
- **EM 385-1-1** — USACE Safety & Health; **40-Hour SSHO** (Site Safety & Health Officer) course + 8-hr annual refresher for federal projects
- **Competent Person** designations — Fall Protection, Excavation/Trenching, Scaffolding, Confined Space
- First Aid / CPR / AED certification
- HAZWOPER (if remediation/hazmat) — OSHA 1910.120
- Cold-weather / Alaska site-specific hazard training

## Recommended
- BCSP **CHST** — Construction Health & Safety Technician
- BCSP **STSC** — Safety Trained Supervisor Construction
- OSHA 500 (Construction Outreach Trainer)
- Crane/rigging, silica, and LOTO competent-person courses

## Nice-to-have
- BCSP **CSP** — Certified Safety Professional
- **ASP** — Associate Safety Professional
- **CIH** — Certified Industrial Hygienist
