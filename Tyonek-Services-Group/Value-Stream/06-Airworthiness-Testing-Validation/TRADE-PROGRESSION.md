# Airworthiness Support, Testing & Validation — Trade Classification & Progression

**Subsidiary:** Tyonek Services Group, Inc.
**Work Process:** Airworthiness Support, Testing & Validation
**Last updated:**

> Maps this job scope to recognized classifications. This is primarily an engineering /
> technician scope (credential- and experience-based) rather than a classic trade apprenticeship.

## Trade Classification
- **Primary occupation:** Aerospace Engineering & Operations Technician (technician tier); Aerospace Engineer (professional tier)
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 17-3021.00 — Aerospace Engineering and Operations Technologists and Technicians
- **Related occupation(s):** 17-2011.00 Aerospace Engineers; 49-3011.00 Aircraft Mechanics (functional test)
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable as **Engineering Technician**; engineer tier is degree + licensure
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=17-3021.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** Moderate

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice / Test Technician** — instrumentation, data acquisition, functional-check support
2. **Journeyman Engineering Technician** — executes ground/flight test procedures, airworthiness data support
3. **Test Engineer / Airworthiness Lead (PE)** — MIL-HDBK-516 assessments, airworthiness release support

## Typical Structure
- Engineering Technician apprenticeship (~4,000–8,000 OJL hrs + RTI) for the technician tier
- Engineer tier: ABET degree → FE/EIT → PE (not apprenticeship)

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Register the technician tier; keep test-engineering as a professional ladder.
- DCMA 8210.1C and MIL-HDBK-516 content from `CERTIFICATIONS.md` anchor the RTI.
