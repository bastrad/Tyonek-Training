## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Aircraft Mechanic (Airframe & Powerplant) — Rotary-Wing
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 49-3011.00 — Aircraft Mechanics and Service Technicians
- **Related occupation(s):** 49-2091.00 Avionics Technicians
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Helicopter/Aircraft Mechanic)
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=49-3011.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice Mechanic** — supervised rotary-wing maintenance, dynamic-component familiarity
2. **Journeyman A&P Mechanic** — independent maintenance, track & balance, return-to-service
3. **Master / Inspector (IA) / Crew Lead** — RII authority, drivetrain/rotor specialization, mentors apprentices

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~8,000 OJL hours (typically 4 years) — aligns with FAA A&P requirements (14 CFR 65)
- On-the-job learning + ~144 hrs/yr Related Technical Instruction (RTI)
- Completion credential: FAA A&P certificate = journey-level
