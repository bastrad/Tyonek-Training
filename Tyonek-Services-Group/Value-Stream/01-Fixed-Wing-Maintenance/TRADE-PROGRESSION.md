## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Aircraft Mechanic (Airframe & Powerplant)
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 49-3011.00 — Aircraft Mechanics and Service Technicians
- **Related occupation(s):** 49-2091.00 Avionics Technicians; 51-2011.00 Aircraft Structure/Systems Assemblers
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Airplane/Aircraft Mechanic)
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=49-3011.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice Mechanic** — supervised maintenance, tooling, tech-data use; building toward A&P eligibility
2. **Journeyman A&P Mechanic** — FAA-certificated, independent maintenance & return-to-service (with Part 145)
3. **Master / Inspector (IA) / Crew Lead** — Required Inspection Items, sign-off authority, mentors apprentices

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~8,000 OJL hours (typically 4 years) — aligns with FAA A&P experience requirements (14 CFR 65)
- On-the-job learning + ~144 hrs/yr Related Technical Instruction (RTI)
- Completion credential: FAA A&P certificate = journey-level

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- The FAA A&P certificate functions as the journeyman credential — an ideal registered apprenticeship anchor.
- C-130 platform qualification and RII from `CERTIFICATIONS.md` define the master/inspector tier.
