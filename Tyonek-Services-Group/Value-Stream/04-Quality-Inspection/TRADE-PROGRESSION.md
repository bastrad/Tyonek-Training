## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Aircraft Inspector
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 49-3011.00 — Aircraft Mechanics and Service Technicians (inspector roles)
- **Related occupation(s):** 53-6051.01 Aviation Inspectors; 51-9061.00 Inspectors/Testers
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Inspector, Aircraft) — typically after journey-level mechanic
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=49-3011.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High (as an advancement from A&P mechanic)

## Progression Path
1. **Journeyman A&P Mechanic** (prerequisite base) — see Fixed/Rotary-Wing Maintenance
2. **Quality Inspector** — Part 145 inspection, RII, buy-back, records verification
3. **Master / Inspection Authorization (IA) / QA Manager** — inspection program authority, auditing, RCCA

## Typical Structure
- Advancement track built on the A&P journey credential; inspector qualification per RSQM
- Related instruction: AS9110, NAS410 NDT, auditing
- Completion credential: designated Part 145 Inspector; FAA IA for the master tier

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Best structured as the inspector rung of the aircraft-mechanic apprenticeship rather than a standalone entry program.
- Aligns to AS9110 auditor and NAS410 NDT items in `CERTIFICATIONS.md`.
