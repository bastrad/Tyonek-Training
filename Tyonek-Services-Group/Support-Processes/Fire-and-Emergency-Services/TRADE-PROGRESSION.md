## Trade Classification
- **Primary trade / occupation:** Fire Fighter (Aircraft Rescue / ARFF)
- **O*NET-SOC code:** 33-2011.00 — Firefighters
- **Related occupation(s):** 33-2021.00 Fire Inspectors & Investigators; 29-2042 EMTs
- **DOL Registered Apprenticeship:** Apprenticeable (Fire Fighter is an established apprenticeable occupation)
- **Occupation finder:** https://www.apprenticeship.gov/apprenticeship-occupations/listings?occupationCode=33-2011.00
- **Apprenticeship suitability:** High

## Progression Path
1. **Apprentice Firefighter** — NFPA 1001 FF I/II, HAZMAT Awareness/Ops, CPR/First Aid
2. **Journey Firefighter (ARFF-qualified)** — NFPA 1003 aircraft rescue, NFPA 409 systems, driver/operator (NFPA 1002)
3. **Fire Officer / Master (NFPA 1021) / Inspector (NFPA 1031)** — command, inspection, instruction (NFPA 1041)

## Typical Apprenticeship Structure
- Term: ~2,000–6,000 OJL hours + Related Technical Instruction (fire academy curriculum)
- Completion credential: journey Firefighter with FF I/II + ARFF

## Company Sponsorship Notes
- Firefighter apprenticeship is well-established and maps cleanly to the NFPA certification ladder in `CERTIFICATIONS.md`.
- ARFF (NFPA 1003) is the differentiator for this aircraft-hangar (NFPA 409) environment.
